Introduction

When one firsts envisions a programming language, there's a high likelihood they may think of something similar looking to C++ or Java. However, this term refers to more than just the familiar high level languages one is most likely to see. To understand what a programming language is, one must first look at the difference between a high level language and low/machine level language. While a compiled format such as C++ may be quite legible to you, your CPU is bound to disagree.

A compiler will take your code and translate it down to machine language, while an interpreted language will utilize its own proprietary program to carry out instructions. In both cases, the majority of coding languages made will translate input to simpler and more managable code, either for the user's machine to follow (compiled) or for the program itself to follow (interpreted). C++, Java, and Python represent a perfect spectrum the varied means by which computers read programs.
